Pot Tamale

Pot Tamale

    Ingredients

    In Panamanian cuisine, tamal de olla, which is Spanish for "tamale of the pot/pan," is best described as a Panamanian-style tamale that fills the baking pan in which it is cooked, and is not wrapped in a banana or plantain leaf.

      Dough

    • 2 cups cornflour
    • 5 cups vegetable stock
    • 6 tablespoons vegetable oil rubbed with achiote
    • to taste Salt and pepper

      • Stuffing

    • 250gr sliced mushrooms
    • 1 large white onion in small cubes
    • 5 finely chopped sweet peppers
    • 1 red pepper, finely julienned
    • 1 green pepper in fine julienne strips
    • 6 coriander leaves
    • 5 cloves garlic, finely chopped
    • 4 tablespoons soy sauce
    • 1/2 cup sliced green olives
    • 1 cup white wine
    • 3 tablespoons achiote oil
    • 2 tablespoons capers, chopped
    • 1 cup vegetable stock
    • Olive oil
    • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Steps

  1. In a saucepan over high heat, pour in the oil, add the garlic, onion and mushrooms. Cook for 5 minutes or until the onions are brown.
  2. Lower the heat to medium and add the rest of the stuffing ingredients and water. Cover the pan and cook for 10 minutes.
  3. Turn off the heat and let the mixture stand.
  4. In a new bowl, add the cornflour, stirring constantly. If necessary, add a little more water.
  5. In an earthenware or porcelain bowl, place a bed of corn dough, cover with the chicken mixture and finally cover with the rest of the dough.
